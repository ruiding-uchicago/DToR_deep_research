- **Durability & fouling control** – Ultra‑light aerogels retain **≥ 89 % compressive strain** after 100 cycles, survive **≥ 18 months** outdoor exposure, and maintain performance with **≤ 5 %** loss after **UVC‑duty‑cycled (≤ 100 mW) antibacterial treatment**; UV‑stable coatings preserve > 90 % efficiency after 10 yr simulated exposure.  
- **Energy‑payback & sustainability** – Low‑temperature carbonisation (≈ 300 °C) cuts embodied energy by **60–70 %** (≈ 0.5 kWh m⁻²) and yields a **2–3 h solar‑energy payback**; the carbon matrix sequesters biomass‑derived carbon, giving a net **CO₂‑avoidance** advantage.

**B. Divergent Claims (statement ↔ counter‑statement)**  

- *Statement:* “The 0.8 V ionic‑gradient alone can sustain continuous faradaic metal recovery without any external power.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Membrane series resistance (≈ 5 Ω) and overpotentials for Zn²⁺/Zn (‑0.76 V) limit practical current density, requiring supplemental bias for high‑throughput metal capture.”  

- *Statement:* “Salt‑resistant micro‑channels completely eliminate crystallisation, enabling indefinite operation in seawater.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Field tests beyond 30 days are lacking; long‑term scaling of salt crystals in stagnant zones may still occur under low‑wind, high‑evaporation conditions.”  

- *Statement:* “UV‑stable nanocoatings guarantee > 90 % thermal efficiency after a decade of outdoor exposure.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Only accelerated‑aging (140 h UV) data are reported; real‑world photodegradation under fluctuating spectra and temperature cycles remains unquantified.”  

- *Statement:* “Conductive polymer skins (σ ≤ 0.5 Ω cm⁻¹) cause negligible electrical loss, making the system fully self‑powered.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Measured power densities (≈ 55 µW cm⁻²) are orders of magnitude lower than the solar thermal flux, limiting practical use to ultra‑low‑power sensors.”  

- *Statement:* “Low‑temperature carbonisation (≈ 300 °C) yields aerogels with identical photothermal performance to high‑temperature (≥ 900 °C) counterparts.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Higher graphitisation at > 800 °C improves conductivity and durability; some low‑temp samples show reduced mechanical resilience after wet‑dry cycling.”  

- *Statement:* “The integrated system can simultaneously treat salts, heavy metals, oils, VOCs, and CO₂ in a single pass.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Experimental demonstrations address each contaminant class separately; a unified multi‑contaminant workflow with quantified removal efficiencies is still missing.”
