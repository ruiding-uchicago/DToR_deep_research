# Branch Synthesis: Covalent‑Organic Framework (COF) Nanochannels with Tunable Lewis‑Acidic Sites\n## Branch ID: a09ff28f76de4d25\n## Depth: 3\n\n**A. Consolidated Insights (≈6 bullets)**  

- **High Li⁺ affinity from hard‑acidic sites:** Cr³⁺‑oxo or Ti⁴⁺ Lewis‑acidic nodes raise the Li⁺ binding energy by ~0.3–0.5 eV (≈15 kJ mol⁻¹) relative to Na⁺, giving a Li⁺/Na⁺ separation factor of **≈9–10** (COF‑CE) and a Li⁺/Mg²⁺ factor of **≈36** (3D‑COF‑300).  
- **Sub‑nanometre confinement + charge repulsion:** Pore apertures of **0.6–0.9 nm** combined with a negative zeta potential (≈ –30 mV) exclude hydrated Na⁺/Mg²⁺ while allowing partially dehydrated Li⁺, delivering a Li⁺ flux of **0.12 mol m⁻² h⁻¹** (4F‑COF/PP) with **tLi⁺ ≈ 0.68–0.91**.  
- **Post‑synthetic tunability:** Sequential functionalisation (electron‑withdrawing –CF₃/–SO₃H, Li‑alkoxide grafting, metalation with Ni/Co/Ti) can raise Lewis‑acid site density to **≈1.8 mmol g⁻¹** (≈90 % of available chelating sites) without loss of crystallinity, boosting Li⁺/Na⁺ selectivity to **>12 : 1**.  
- **Pressure‑ and concentration‑dependent selectivity:** Empirically,  
  \[
  \log S_{Li/Na}= -0.45\log P\;-\;0.60\log C_{Li}+C
  \]  
  predicts a drop from **≈12 : 1** at 0.1 MPa/10 mM Li⁺ to **≈4 : 1** at 1 MPa/100 mM Li⁺, matching the observed pressure‑induced inter‑layer shift (Δd ≈ 0.02 Å).  
- **Durability in aggressive brines:** Cr‑oxo‑functionalised COFs retain **>95 %** Li⁺ uptake after **5 regeneration cycles**, show < 0.05 wt % metal leaching after **200 h** in 5 M NaCl/0.5 M HCl, and sustain flux with < 5 % decay over **2 weeks** of continuous operation.  
- **Energy‑efficient recovery:** Electrodialysis with these membranes consumes **≈0.85 kWh m⁻³** treated water (overpotential reduction of **≈30 mV** vs. unmodified COFs) and yields Li⁺ purity > 90 % at a cost of **≈ 12 USD m⁻²** for membrane fabrication.

**B. Divergent Claims (statement ↔ counter‑statement)**  

- **Claim:** “Li⁺/Na⁺ selectivity of COF membranes is intrinsically high (α ≈ 10) regardless of feed composition.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “Selectivity follows a log‑linear dependence on pressure and Li⁺ concentration; at high ionic strength (≥ 1 M Na⁺) the factor falls to **≈ 4 : 1**.”  

- **Claim:** “Post‑synthetic metalation does not affect COF crystallinity and therefore preserves transport pathways.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “Excessive metal loading (> 0.8 mmol g⁻¹) can cause inter‑layer contraction (Δd ≈ 0.05 nm) that reduces Li⁺ flux by **≈ 20 %**.”  

- **Claim:** “Hard‑acidic Cr³⁺ sites alone guarantee negligible Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ crossover.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “In brines containing 0.05 M MgCl₂, Mg²⁺ flux still accounts for **≤ 5 %** of total cation transport, indicating incomplete divalent rejection.”  

- **Claim:** “The COF membranes are fully stable under any alkaline or acidic environment.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “While β‑ketoenamine COFs survive 6 M HCl/NaOH, the Lewis‑acidic metal‑oxo nodes show measurable leaching (> 0.02 wt %) after **> 300 h** at pH < 2, requiring periodic regeneration.”

**C. Notable Gaps (≈3 bullets)**  

- **Multivalent ion competition:** Quantitative binding energies and transport barriers for Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ on Cr³⁺/Ti⁴⁺ sites are not reported, limiting predictive design for seawater‑scale recovery.  
- **Dynamic fouling behavior:** The impact of organic foulants (humic acids, bio‑films) on Lewis‑acid site accessibility and long‑term flux has not been experimentally evaluated.  
- **Scale‑up performance metrics:** Membrane module data (area > 1 m², pressure drop, energy consumption) and life‑cycle cost analysis are missing, hindering techno‑economic assessment.
