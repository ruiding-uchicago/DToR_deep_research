- Template-guided orientational growth is enabled by ultra-high seed densities (>10^12 cm−2) from polymer-assisted dip-seeding and nanodiamond seeds, applied to appropriate solid supports (e.g., Si3N4-capped substrates) with a Ga–Ni–Fe–Si melt interface. - Melt-surface engineering, including a thin barrier layer (Si2N2O or Si3N4, 3–5 nm), reduces melt-back at pattern edges and maintains manageable static contact angles (20–30°). Interfacial engineering thus emerges as a central lever to preserve pattern integrity while enabling scalable deposition.
- A scalable, wafer-level workflow is feasible with spin-coating of deposition templates (e.g., PDDAC) that yield thickness variations below 10% on 100 mm wafers. - Stress management emerges as a critical determinant of large-area/volume crystal quality. Raman-calibrated stress mapping reveals a linear relation with thickness for film stress, with a solvable tensile bias from substrate creep. The use of a thin interlayer (e.g., ~5 nm SiC) can push residual stress toward near-neutral values, enabling thicker films without delamination or cracking during thermal cycling. In large-area contexts, controlling biaxial stress (< tens of MPa range) is essential for maintaining crystal coherence across a wide area.

Resolution: Use time-resolved ion flux control, short high-energy pulses with rest periods, and interleaved low-energy growth steps to minimize sub-surface damage; monitor defect densities with NV ODMR and adjust exposure accordingly.
- NV-ensemble closed-loop latency vs rapid plasma transients. NV-based feedback aims for sub-100 µs latency, but rapid plasma transients (pulses >300 s) could create magnetic-axis hot spots that exceed tolerance. Resolution: Implement hierarchical feedback with a fast NV-based core loop for high-frequency stabilization and a slower, coarse plasma-control loop for larger transients; incorporate predictive models to anticipate and damp transients prior to NV sensing limits.
- Orientation uniformity vs pattern fidelity at scale. While patterned melt templating shows strong orientation control at lab scale, edge-edge Ni/Fe enrichment uniformity on large wafers can vary (±2 at% Ni/Fe), raising questions about uniformity of nucleation and texture across a 100 mm to 300 mm substrate. Resolution: Extend patterning recipes to wafer-scale deposition with uniform pattern transfer, use diffusion barriers or graded barrier coatings to equalize melt-front behavior, and employ comprehensive XPS/XRD scattering maps to quantify edge-to-center variations for feedback.
- Stress optimization vs thermal-cycling fatigue in large-area stacks. The interlayer design (e.g., 5 nm SiC) helps reduce residual stress, but large-area structures undergo many thermal cycles which can generate localized tensile hotspots or delamination if geometry is not optimized. Resolution: Couple microfluidic-style cooling channels and stress-relieving interlayers to distribute heat and reduce local gradients; validate through cyclic thermal tests and in situ Raman mapping across full-area samples.
