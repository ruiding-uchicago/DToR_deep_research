# Branch Synthesis: Programmable selectivity and anti-fouling via hybrid MXene/COF/polymer nanochannels\n## Branch ID: 105af9c840eb701d\n## Depth: 3\n\n**A. Consolidated Insights (≈6 bullets)**  

- Hybrid MXene/COF/polymer nano‑channels deliver **ultra‑high water fluxes** (up to **643 200 L m⁻² h⁻¹ bar⁻¹** for water‑in‑oil emulsions) while maintaining **> 99 % contaminant rejection** (dyes, BPA, PFAS, salts).  
- **Electro‑photo gating** provides a linear, reversible pore‑size modulation of **≈ 0.5 nm V⁻¹** (±5 V bias) plus a thermal swelling of **2–5 nm (W cm⁻²)⁻¹** under NIR illumination, enabling on‑demand selectivity shifts (K⁺/Na⁺ selectivity 1 → 4.6, divalent‑ion rejection ≈ 80 %).  
- **In‑situ ROS nanoreactors** (Co₃O₄@MXene, Co‑MXene) achieve **100 % BPA removal** within **30 min** and **≥ 70 % fouling reduction** after a 5‑min NIR burst, providing self‑cleaning without chemical additives.  
- **Anti‑fouling is reinforced** by MXene’s super‑hydrophilicity (contact angle < 10°), Ag⁺ antibacterial release (99 % *E. coli* kill), and polymer brushes (PEG/PAAm) that suppress protein adsorption by **> 90 %**; flux recovery after fouling exceeds **98 %** with a low‑voltage (‑8 V) sweep.  
- **Mechanical durability** is demonstrated by MXene‑polymer double‑network hydrogels sustaining **> 360 h** continuous operation, super‑elastic MXene aerogels surviving **> 3000** bending cycles, and electro‑photo cycles exceeding **10⁴** with < 5 % performance loss.  
- **Scalable manufacturing** is feasible via roll‑to‑roll Meyer‑rod or slot‑die coating (≥ 5 m sheet length, 5 m min⁻¹ speed) and pressure‑assisted filtration, yielding uniform interlayer spacing **± 2 nm** and material utilization > 95 % (raw‑material waste < 5 %).

**B. Divergent Claims (statement vs. counter‑statement)**  

- **Claim:** MXene loading above **1 mg cm⁻²** dramatically improves conductivity and flux.  
  **Counter‑statement:** Loadings ≥ 2 mg cm⁻² increase surface roughness, causing a **10–15 % flux decline** after 10 h due to fouling.  

- **Claim:** Voltage‑driven pore contraction of **0.8–2.5 nm** (≤ 1 V) yields a stable “closed” state with high ion selectivity.  
  **Counter‑statement:** Excessive bias (±5 V) can induce MXene oxidation, reducing conductivity by **≈ 30 %** after 8 days of ambient exposure.  

- **Claim:** NIR‑induced photothermal heating (≤ 5 W cm⁻²) provides rapid fouling removal and flux restoration > 85 % after 10 cycles.  
  **Counter‑statement:** Continuous high‑power NIR (> 5 W cm⁻²) raises membrane temperature > 70 °C, risking polymer degradation and loss of mechanical integrity after **≈ 200 h**.  

- **Claim:** Incorporating Ag‑functionalised MXene gives long‑term antibacterial protection (≥ 90 days).  
  **Counter‑statement:** Ag⁺ leaching can exceed regulatory limits (> 0.01 mg L⁻¹) in high‑flow systems, necessitating additional barrier layers.

**C. Notable Gaps (≈3 bullets)**  

- **Long‑term field durability:** No published data on continuous operation > 12 months under industrial pressures (≥ 1 MPa) and real‑world fouling mixtures (high salinity + oil + microbes).  
- **Energy balance of electro‑photo cycles:** Specific energy consumption (kWh m⁻³) for combined voltage bias and NIR heating, especially at scale, remains **‑ not reported**.  
- **Selective removal of multivalent/trace metals:** Quantitative rejection rates for ions such as Pb²⁺, Cd²⁺, Sr²⁺, and Li⁺ are absent, limiting assessment for advanced water‑recovery applications.
