- **Electro‑photo gating delivers a reproducible flux boost (≈ 1.8 ×) and > 90 % flux‑recovery after each cleaning cycle**, thanks to the combined effect of a low‑voltage bias (‑2 to ‑8 V) that repels foulants and visible‑light‑activated TiO₂‑WO₃/GO (or Ti/W) photocatalysts that generate reactive oxygen species for in‑situ self‑cleaning.  
- **Durability is proven at multiple scales:** laboratory tests show > 99 % activity after 5 × 7 h UV/vis cycles, pilot‑scale EMBR/EMF runs maintain > 93 % COD and > 96 % NH₄⁺‑N removal for > 475 days, and conductive‑backing membranes retain mechanical integrity for > 2 yr of cyclic bias reversal.

- **Scalable illumination is achievable:** solar‑radiation‑resistant 200 µm silica‑fiber bundles (≤ 0.1 dB h⁻¹ ageing after the first 30 min) and high‑efficiency UV‑LED arrays (10–12 W, 255–405 nm) can be retro‑fitted into existing hollow‑fiber or spiral‑wound modules, enabling > 30 % higher packing density and uniform photon delivery up to 10 m³ h⁻¹.  
- **Smart‑control (IoT/AI) closes the loop:** real‑time sensors (TMP, UV intensity, flux) coupled with predictive models (R² = 0.93 for flux decline) modulate LED duty‑cycle and bias polarity, cutting illumination energy by up to 12 % and extending LED life by ~10 %.

**C. Notable Gaps (≈ 3 bullets)**

- **End‑of‑life recycling pathways:** Life‑cycle inventories lack detailed accounting for LED chip, fiber‑optic, and Ti/W nanocomposite recycling, limiting the robustness of the reported GWP savings.
