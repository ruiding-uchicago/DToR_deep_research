- 2‑D probe platforms (BP‑FET, MoS₂/APT/CS, enzymatic BiVO₄, β‑lactamase colorimetry, MOF‑based fluorescence, CQD sensors) consistently achieve **sub‑nanomolar LODs** (0.18 nM ≈ 0.05 µg L⁻¹) for β‑lactams and **≤ 10 ng mL⁻¹** for aminoglycosides, meeting or exceeding regulatory detection thresholds for wastewater.  
- **Selectivity** is governed by the recognition layer: enzymatic hydrolysis or β‑lactam‑specific MIPs suppress non‑β‑lactam interference to **≤ 1 %** signal change, while MoS₂/APT/CS electrochemical sensors report a **selectivity coefficient of 12.8** against five common interferents.  
- **Matrix‑effect mitigation** relies on (i) **temperature/pH‑compensated digital pH electrodes**, (ii) **matrix‑matched calibration curves**, and (iii) **oil‑film (QSF) lubrication** that eliminates water ingress and stabilises probe housings in high‑salinity, high‑turbidity streams.  
- **Operational durability** varies: enzymatic probes retain full activity for **≈ 28 days** at 4 °C (loss after 16 days at ambient), MOF‑IITKGP‑71 remains chemically stable for **3 months** (pH 3–12), while most electro‑/optical platforms are presently **single‑use** or survive **≤ 2 regeneration cycles**.  
- **Real‑time compensation** (dual‑channel SPR salinity sensors, ultrasonic film‑thickness monitors, ML‑driven drift correction) enables **sub‑second response** (≤ 6 s) and maintains **≤ 5 %** bias across **pH 5–9**, **ionic strength 0–0.2 M**, and **turbidity 0–50 NTU**.  
- **Scalability & field integration** have been demonstrated: QSF‑lubricated housings (> 50 mm) sustain continuous flow (5–10 L min⁻¹) with oil‑rejection > 99 %; membrane‑distillation modules deliver **2043 L m⁻² h⁻¹** flux and retain **> 90 %** oil rejection after 10 cycles, providing a practical platform for long‑term deployment of 2‑D antibiotic sensors.

**C. Notable Gaps (≈ 3 bullets)**  

- **Quantitative long‑term fouling data:** No field‑scale studies reporting bio‑film thickness, lubricant loss, or sensor drift beyond **30–60 days** for QSF‑lubricated housings and 2‑D probes.  
- **Standardised matrix‑effect protocol:** Absence of a universally adopted spiking‑level (low/medium/high) and recovery reporting framework for **≥ 5 antibiotic classes** in real wastewater, hindering cross‑study comparability.  
- **Regeneration and reuse metrics:** Limited experimental evidence on multi‑cycle chemical regeneration (e.g., SDS cleaning, enzymatic re‑activation) for MoS₂, BP‑FET, and MOF platforms; most reports remain single‑use.
