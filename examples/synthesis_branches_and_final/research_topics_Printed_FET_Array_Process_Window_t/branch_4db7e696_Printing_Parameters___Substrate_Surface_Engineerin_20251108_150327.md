# Branch Synthesis: Printing Parameters & Substrate Surface Engineering\n## Branch ID: 4db7e6967e537dc8\n## Depth: 3\n\n**A. Consolidated Insights (≈ 6 bullets)**  

- A 1️⃣ A sub‑10 nm rms roughness (≤ 12 nm on paper, ≤ 5 nm on SiO₂) is the single most decisive lever for preserving high OFET mobility (0.9 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹ for organic, 0.24 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹ for MoS₂) and limiting Vₜₕ drift; every 10 nm increase in rms cuts mobility by ~1 decade (μ ∝ e⁻ᵅʳᵐˢ², α≈0.03–0.07 nm⁻²).  

- A 2️⃣ A “geometry‑percolation window” links active‑layer thickness and dopant loading: 20‑80 nm (small‑molecule) or 30‑100 nm (polymer) films with ≤ 1 wt % weak dopant (TFP, OFN) or ≤ 0.5 wt % strong dopant (F₄TCNQ, BCF) reliably yields μ > 10 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹ and on/off > 10⁴.  

- A 3️⃣ Surface‑energy engineering via SAMs (OTS, ODTS, FOTS) that match the preferred crystal facet (tilt ≈ 40‑48°, thickness ≈ 6‑17 Å) maximizes nucleation density, reduces low‑frequency noise, and enables contact‑resistivity < 10⁻⁴ Ω cm² when combined with fine‑patterned strong dopant at the source/drain.  

- A 4️⃣ Printing‑parameter control (viscosity ≈ 10 cP ± 2 cP, drop spacing ≈ 30 µm, substrate temperature ≈ 60 °C, post‑print anneal ≈ 80 °C) keeps the capillary number near unity, suppresses coffee‑ring formation, and produces uniform fill‑factors (FF ≥ 0.5) that linearly scale mobility from ≈ 1 → 16 cm² V⁻¹ s⁻¹ for organic networks.  

- A 5️⃣ Hybrid substrate stacks (25 nm Al₂O₃ + 0.2 nm OTS/ODTS) lower rms to ≤ 0.30 nm, halve the roughness‑scattering constant (α ≈ 0.35 vs. 1.0 on SiO₂) and limit mobility loss to < 5 % for ±0.2 µm dimensional variations, while preserving high‑k capacitance (Cᵢ ≈ 2.4 × 10⁻⁷ F cm⁻²).  

- A 6️⃣ Mechanical and environmental robustness is achieved when nanogroove depth ≈ 0.5 × film thickness, polymer‑elastomer transition layers match the OSC modulus, and encapsulation (30 nm Al₂O₃ + 1 µm parylene ± catechol adhesive) limits ΔVₜₕ < ±0.5 V after 6 months at 80 % RH and > 10⁴ bending cycles at 50 % strain.

- B 1️⃣ *Statement:* “Ion‑gel gating completely eliminates roughness‑induced mobility loss up to rms = 50 nm.” ↔ *Counter‑statement:* “Roughness‑insensitive HIFETs still show a measurable α ≈ 0.03 nm⁻²; mobility begins to degrade beyond rms ≈ 30 nm.”
