- **Ultra‑low‑cost base platform:** Printable Au/SiO₂ impedance electrodes can be produced for **≈ AUD 0.25 (~ US $0.18)** each; roll‑to‑roll (R2R) and ink‑jet/FDM additive manufacturing keep the total bill‑of‑materials for a disposable probe under **US $0.10 /unit**.  
- **High‑gain add‑on:** A **5 nm MoS₂** (or other 2‑D TMD) SPR layer adds a **12‑fold** (≈ 260–739 %) sensitivity boost, allowing a premium “high‑gain” module that still costs **< US $0.01 per sensor** in material.  
- **Scalable 2‑D material supply:** Continuous‑flow liquid‑phase exfoliation (ball‑mill + CO₂ supercritical) delivers **≥ 5 kg day⁻¹** of > 99.99 % pure graphene, MoS₂, WS₂ and Te flakes; bulk contracts and a shared NMP‑recovery plant cut solvent cost to **US $1.12 kg⁻¹** and reduce OPEX by **≈ 15–20 %**.  
- **Encapsulation & circularity:** Recycled‑TPU/Flexdym elastomer housings (≤ US $0.10 kg⁻¹) and an Aceretech ASP line achieve **> 95 %** overall material recovery (polymer + nanofiller) and a **≈ 90 %** drop in embodied energy, delivering **> 2 kg CO₂ eq kg⁻¹** carbon avoidance.

- **Supply‑chain resilience:** Dual‑sourcing of precursors, 30 % Cyrene‑NMP green solvent blend, and on‑site Te melt‑quench eliminate single‑point failures; risk‑matrix modelling shows a **≈ 5‑fold** reduction in disruption‑related cost spikes.

**B. Divergent Claims (statement vs. counter‑statement)**  

- **Sensitivity boost magnitude** – *Statement 1*: a 5 nm MoS₂ layer yields a **12‑fold** (≈ 1 200 %) sensitivity increase (Report 1).  
  *Counter‑statement*: experimental data on engineered Al‑doped WO₃ report a **260–739 %** (≈ 2.6–7.4 ×) boost, suggesting the MoS₂ effect may be over‑estimated in some contexts (Report 4).  

- **Nanofiller retention after recycling** – *Statement 1*: the Aceretech ASP line recovers **> 95 %** of total polymer and nanofiller mass (Report 9).  
  *Counter‑statement*: CNT mass after **5 recycling cycles** drops to **73 %**, with a 1.37× resistance increase, indicating that specific nanofiller recovery can be substantially lower than the bulk figure (Report 9).  

- **Cost of TPU filament** – *Statement 1*: recycled TPU filament can be produced for **≤ US $0.10 kg⁻¹**, a > 99 % raw‑material cost cut versus virgin TPU (**$20–$175 kg⁻¹**) (Report 9).  
  *Counter‑statement*: some pilot studies still quote **US $0.05–$0.15 kg⁻¹** for high‑purity Covestro CQ‑TPU, implying that the ultra‑low figure may not be universally achievable without additional processing steps (Report 9).
