# Branch Synthesis: Inorganic Nanoporous Frameworks (Zeolites, MOFs, LDHs) as Size‑Selective Ion‑Exchange Membranes\n## Branch ID: 85f41ea2f48e7b9f\n## Depth: 3\n\n**A. Consolidated Insights**  

- **“Double‑multiplying” sieving** – Stacking a sub‑nanometre zeolite top‑coat on a MOF (e.g., ZIF‑8/zeolite‑T) raises gas‑phase selectivity from ≈ 4 : 1 to ≈ 229 : 1 and, by analogy, sharpens the Li⁺ (0.76 Å) vs. Co²⁺/Ni²⁺ (≈ 0.90 Å) cut‑off, delivering Li⁺/Co²⁺ selectivity factors of **30–60** in aqueous tests.  
- **Rigid‑capping with conductive nanophases** – Embedding WC nanoclusters (σ ≈ 6.3 × 10⁷ S m⁻¹) or Ag nanoparticles in ZIF‑8 suppresses framework breathing, fixes the effective aperture (Δr ≤ 0.05 nm) under bias, and simultaneously provides electronic pathways for electro‑chemical regeneration.  
- **LDH interlayer tuning** – ZnAl‑NO₃ or Mg/Al LDH sheets with gallery heights 0.8–1.0 nm act as a second sub‑nanometre filter; they admit hydrated Li⁺ (≈ 3.4 Å) while imposing a ≥ 30 % diffusion penalty on multivalent Co²⁺, Ni²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe³⁺.  
- **Surface‑charge engineering** – Positive nanolayers (PEI, quaternary‑ammonium‑modified PA) achieving surface charge densities > +5 mC m⁻² generate Donnan exclusion that pushes Mg²⁺/Ni²⁺/Fe³⁺ rejection > 99 % and boosts Li⁺/Mg²⁺ selectivity to **37–60** without sacrificing water flux (≈ 55–56 L m⁻² h⁻¹).  
- **Ion‑specific functionalisation** – Phosphonic‑acid grafting on zeolite 5A (1.0–1.2 mmol g⁻¹ IEC) and Co‑doping of ZIF‑8 (2–10 % Co) raise Co²⁺ binding energy by ~15 % while preserving the Li⁺ size window, enabling > 80 % Co capture in a single pass.  
- **Long‑term durability cues** – ZIF‑8‑coated LDH composites survive **10 000** charge‑discharge cycles (92.6 % capacitance retention) and modelling predicts polycrystalline MOF layers can endure > 1000 h of cyclic pressure without fracture, suggesting the architecture can meet industrial‑scale service life.

**B. Divergent Claims**  

- *Statement:* “A single‑stage membrane can achieve Li⁺/Co²⁺ selectivity > 100, sufficient for commercial Li recovery.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Mechanistic modelling shows a single stage requires S > 100, yet the highest experimentally reported S is ≈ 60; cascades or further amplification are still needed.”  

- *Statement:* “Framework breathing is negligible once WC nanoclusters are added; pore size remains fixed under all operating voltages.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Operando neutron/X‑ray studies still detect sub‑angstrom Δr (≤ 0.05 nm) under 0.3 V mm⁻¹ bias, indicating residual breathing that can affect selectivity.”  

- *Statement:* “Positive surface charge alone guarantees > 99 % rejection of all multivalent cations.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Experiments with high Na⁺/K⁺ background (brine II) show Li⁺/Mg²⁺ selectivity dropping from 17.3 to 7.9, revealing charge screening and concentration‑polarisation effects that erode multivalent rejection.”  

**C. Notable Gaps**  

- **> 1000 h continuous ion‑exchange data** for realistic Li⁺/Co²⁺/Mg²⁺/Ni²⁺ brines; current durability evidence is limited to ≤ 24 h or electrochemical cycling proxies.  
- **Quantitative multicomponent competition models** that predict fluxes and selectivity for five‑ion mixtures (Li⁺, Co²⁺, Ni²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe³⁺) are absent; existing work reports only binary or ternary selectivity factors.  
- **Energy‑consumption accounting** for pressure‑driven or electrically‑biased operation (e.g., required trans‑membrane voltage, pumping power) has not been measured, hindering techno‑economic assessment.
