# Branch Synthesis: Interfacial engineering and Li-metal compatibility with receptor-bearing fluorinated ethers\n## Branch ID: 7aa2c8cb6bb7f800\n## Depth: 3\n\n**A. Consolidated Insights (≈ 6 bullets)**  

- **LiF‑rich SEI is the decisive stabiliser** – Operando XPS, cryo‑TEM and ¹⁹F MAS‑NMR consistently show that > 70 % of the SEI fluorine is present as LiF (F 1s ≈ 685 eV, ¹⁹F ≈ ‑207 ppm) after ≤ 200 cycles, and that a thin (5–12 nm) LiF layer reduces charge‑transfer resistance by > 80 % (R_ct ≈ ‑ not reported) while preserving Li⁺ transport (grain‑boundary Ea ≈ 0.21 eV, bulk Ea ≈ 0.55–0.65 eV).  

- **Partial fluorination of ether solvents (e.g., 5FDEE, 11‑5/11‑6 series) gives the optimal balance** – Viscosity ≈ 1.2–1.5 cP, ionic conductivity 3–4 mS cm⁻¹, oxidative stability > 4.6 V, and a self‑limited HF generation (< 10 ppm per cycle). Cells with 5FDEE + 10 % FEC deliver > 550 cycles (2.7–4.3 V) with average Coulombic efficiency > 99.9 %.  

- **Fluoride‑binding receptor L (and its Ni/Co‑chelated analogues) exhibits ultra‑high affinity for F⁻** – Benesi‑Hildebrand fluorescence titrations give Ka = 0.6–1.5 × 10⁸ M⁻¹ (free L) and up to 10⁷ M⁻¹ for the R₁–R₅ series; the binding is 1:2 (L·(F⁻)₂) with a calculated ΔE ≈ ‑150 kJ mol⁻¹ (PM6), > 30 kJ mol⁻¹ stronger than for Cl⁻. This enables rapid capture of the F⁻ released from fluorinated ethers and its conversion to LiF at the Li surface.  

- **Covalent grafting of L onto fluorinated ether backbones provides a self‑healing fluoride source** – Operando ⁹F NMR shows a fluoride release rate of ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ mol F⁻ cm⁻² · cycle⁻¹ (≈ 0.1–0.2 % LiF · cycle⁻¹), which matches the observed LiF growth (0.125 % LiF · cycle⁻¹). The resulting SEI remains thin (≤ 20 nm) and conductive (σ ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻³ S cm⁻¹).  

- **High‑tLi⁺ polymer electrolytes synergise with the receptor‑engineered SEI** – TFOA‑cross‑linked GPE (σ ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻³ S cm⁻¹, tLi⁺ = 0.61) and PFVS networks (tLi⁺ = 0.82) support > 2000 h Li‑metal plating/stripping at 0.2 mA cm⁻², and when combined with the LiF‑rich SEI they suppress dendrite nucleation up to > 4 mA cm⁻².  

- **Scalable grafting routes are already demonstrated** – Continuous‑flow RAFT click chemistry, roll‑to‑roll diazonium electro‑reduction, melt‑processable fluorinated poly(aryl ether)s and plasma‑enhanced fluorination all achieve kilogram‑scale throughput (≤ 24 h for 1 kg, material loss < 5 %) at a total cost of $35–$60 kg⁻¹ (ether $30–$50 kg⁻¹ + $5–$10 kg⁻¹ processing).

**B. Divergent Claims (statement ↔ counter‑statement)**  

- **Claim:** “Fully fluorinated electrolytes (FEC/FEMC/HFE, 2:6:2) give the highest LiF fraction (> 90 %) and thus the most stable SEI.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “Excessive LiF (> 70 nm total thickness) raises Li⁺ migration barriers (Ea ≈ 0.65 eV) and leads to > 30 % impedance growth after ≈ 300 cycles, limiting rate capability.”  

- **Claim:** “The fluoride‑binding receptor L alone is sufficient to suppress HF formation to < 10 ppm, eliminating the need for external HF scavengers.”  
  **Counter‑claim:** “Long‑term calendar ageing (6 months at 45 °C) still shows HF accumulation (~5 ppm) unless an Al₂O₃‑based Janus separator is employed, indicating that receptor capture is not fully exhaustive under realistic moisture ingress.”  

- **Claim:** “High‑tLi⁺ polymer electrolytes (tLi⁺ ≥ 0.8) guarantee uniform Li⁺ flux and prevent dendrite formation at any practical current density.”  
  **Counter‑statement:** “When the polymer matrix is overly fluorinated (PFVS), the bulk ionic conductivity drops below 1 mS cm⁻¹, and dendrites appear at > 2 mA cm⁻² despite the high transference number, showing that conductivity must be co‑optimised.”

**C. Notable Gaps (≈ 3 bullets)**  

- **Quantitative HF‑tracking beyond 1000 cycles** – Operando HF sensors have only been demonstrated up to ≈ 600 cycles; the long‑term evolution of fluoride release and its impact on SEI thickness remain unquantified.  
- **Mechanical robustness of the hybrid LiF + Li₃N SEI under thermal cycling** – While grain‑boundary Li⁺ transport is known (Ea ≈ 0.21 eV), the fracture toughness and adhesion of the mixed SEI across –20 °C → +60 °C have not been measured.  
- **Scalable integration of Janus Al₂O₃/HKUST‑1 separators** – Laboratory‑scale fabrication shows HF reduction, but roll‑to‑roll production, cost analysis, and compatibility with high‑energy cathodes (> 4.5 V) are still missing.
