- **High‑efficiency photothermal evaporation** – 3‑D MOF‑based platforms routinely achieve 1.9–4.1 kg m⁻² h⁻¹ water flux under 1 sun, with apparent solar‑to‑steam efficiencies of 90 %–112 % (e.g., MOF/PVDF/MF composite, ZIF‑8/CNT/gelatin, CFG multilayer).  
- **Low‑enthalpy water transport** – Radical‑linker MOFs (e.g., PFC‑771) and Ni₃(HITP)₂ lower the evaporation enthalpy to ≈ 1 700 J g⁻¹ (≈ 30 % reduction vs. bulk water), enabling the high fluxes above without excessive heating.  
- **Multi‑ion selectivity & capture** – Anionic 3‑D Co‑MOF‑1, imprinted MIPAF‑11c, and Al³⁺/Ga³⁺‑defect‑rich Ga₂O₃ provide ultrahigh UO₂²⁺ uptake (≈ 35 mg g⁻¹) and selectivity coefficients > 700 in full‑strength seawater; crown‑ether‑functionalized MOFs give Li⁺/Na⁺ selectivity > 10⁴.  
- **Integrated catalytic degradation** – Co‑Fc/GO and Fe‑MOF‑EDTA layers generate •OH radicals, delivering > 95 % removal of MB, tetracycline, phenol and complete VOC suppression during evaporation.  
- **Regeneration via magnetic‑MISA or light‑triggered desorption** – Fe₃O₄‑decorated Mg‑MOF‑74 (MFCs) achieves CO₂‑assisted heating for rapid ion‑release; AuBP@UIO‑66 enables on‑demand photodesorption, preserving > 95 % capacity over ≥ 10 cycles.  
- **Techno‑economic & LCA promise** – Material cost ≈ ¥5–6 piece⁻¹ (≈ $0.03 cm⁻³), energy demand 0.9–1.2 kWh m⁻³ (≈ 60 % lower than conventional thermal desalination), and solvent‑recycling can cut GHG intensity by > 30 %.  

**B. Divergent Claims (statement vs. counter‑statement)**  

- *Statement:* “The MXene‑MOF aerogel can achieve near‑100 % salt rejection while maintaining high flux.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Finite‑element modeling shows that for arrays > 1 m the vapor re‑condenses on downstream fins, reducing net flux by > 15 % despite high salt rejection.”  

- *Statement:* “Ion‑selective MOF membranes (Li⁺/Na⁺ > 10⁴) are ready for integration with solar evaporators.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “No experimental platform has yet demonstrated simultaneous discrimination of ≥ 3 cations and ≥ 2 anions under continuous solar operation; selectivity drops below 10³ when competing anions are present.”  

- *Statement:* “Magnetic MISA regeneration consumes negligible electricity because heating is localized.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Quantitative measurements of coil power draw under real‑sun conditions are missing; preliminary tests suggest 0.3–0.5 W m⁻², which could offset the low‑energy advantage.”  

- *Statement:* “Roll‑to‑roll dip‑coating of ZIF‑8/CNT films can be scaled to multi‑meter widths without performance loss.”  
  *Counter‑statement:* “Uniformity of pore‑size distribution degrades beyond 0.5 m width, leading to a 10 % drop in evaporation rate and increased fouling.”  

**C. Notable Gaps (≈ 3 bullets)**
